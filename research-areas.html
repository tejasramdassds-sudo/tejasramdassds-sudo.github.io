Research Areas

Strategy, Organization Theory, Technology Innovation, and AI

Tejas Ramdas is a Cornell PhD candidate in Management and Statistics whose research connects strategy, organization theory, technology and innovation, organizational economics, networks, computational social science, machine learning, AI, and econometrics.

His work asks how firms search over technological space, how enabling and generative technologies reshape competition, and how firms move into knowledge domains associated with other firms' prior strengths. The research combines strategy theory with large-scale patent data, text-as-data methods, network analysis, causal inference, and statistical modeling.
