Innovation Search

Innovation Search and Inventive Activity

Tejas Ramdas studies innovation search: how firms move through technological space, recombine knowledge, and direct inventive effort beyond their established knowledge bases. This work develops the idea of search incursion, where a firm moves toward knowledge elements associated with other firms' prior strengths.

The research connects innovation search, technological proximity, generative inventions, patents, firm strategy, organization theory, computational social science, and econometrics.

Working Paper

Generative Inventions and Search Incursions

The working paper with Gautam Ahuja examines how generative inventions expand conditions under which firms can undertake search incursion, and how technological proximity shapes both use and exposure in follow-on inventive activity.
