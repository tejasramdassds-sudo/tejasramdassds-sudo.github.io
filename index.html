Research Profile

Strategy research with a computational spine.

My work examines how firms search, learn, and compete around emerging technologies. I combine strategy theory with large-scale patent data, text-as-data methods, machine learning, causal inference, and econometric design.

For Search Committees and Collaborators

At the intersection of strategy, organization theory, and AI.

My academic profile sits between strategy PhD research, organization theory, technology and innovation, and computational social science. I study how firms use generative inventions and enabling technologies, how technological proximity shapes inventive search, and how large-scale text and network data can reveal competitive movement before product-market entry.
